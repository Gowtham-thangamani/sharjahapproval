About RTA Sharjah Approvals

The Sharjah Roads and Transport Authority (RTA Sharjah) governs roads, access points, parking, and transportation infrastructure across the emirate. Any project that affects a road, creates a new entrance or access, or impacts traffic flow requires RTA Sharjah permits and traffic clearances before works can proceed.

Our experienced team prepares traffic impact studies, access and entrance designs, and parking arrangements, then manages the full RTA Sharjah submission and approval process on your behalf, ensuring compliance with the authority's road and transportation standards.

RTA Sharjah Approval Scope

  • Road Crossing PermitPermits for cutting, crossing, or working within road reserves for utilities, services, and connections.
  • Access / Entrance ApprovalApproval for new or modified site entrances, driveways, and access points onto public roads.
  • Parking NOCNo-objection certificates for on-site parking layouts, counts, and arrangements as required by RTA Sharjah.
  • Traffic Impact AssessmentTraffic impact studies evaluating how a development affects surrounding roads and junctions.
  • Road Works & Diversion PermitPermits for temporary road works, lane closures, and traffic diversions during construction.

Importance of RTA Sharjah Approval

RTA Sharjah approvals safeguard road safety and guarantee legal, sanctioned access to your site. Securing the correct permits and traffic clearances prevents stop-work orders, ensures smooth traffic management during construction, and keeps roads and junctions functioning safely once the project is complete. Our expert handling ensures compliance and faster approval timelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does RTA Sharjah regulate?

The Sharjah Roads and Transport Authority (RTA Sharjah) governs roads, access points, parking and transportation infrastructure across the emirate. Any project affecting a road, creating a new access, or impacting traffic requires its permits.

What approvals does RTA Sharjah issue?

RTA Sharjah issues road crossing permits, access and entrance approvals, parking NOCs, traffic impact assessments, and road works and diversion permits. We prepare the studies and manage the full submission process.

When is a traffic impact assessment needed?

A traffic impact assessment is required when a development affects surrounding roads and junctions. It evaluates how the project changes traffic flow and is submitted to RTA Sharjah as part of the approval.
